Icosahedron Sculpture

A Story of Dedication, Passion, and Innovation

In May 2015, the Math Alliance Club, led by Jonathan Doane, embarked on a mission to give back to the Mathematics Department at SUNY Potsdam. The result was a stunning icosahedron sculpture, symbolizing both mathematical beauty and the collective effort of a passionate group of individuals. This project stands as a testament to the power of dedication, innovation, and community collaboration.

Pictured: Jonathan Doane and sculptor Alicia Quijano (formerly Alicia Conway)

Inspiration and Conception

SUNY Potsdam's Math Alliance, May 2015

The idea for the icosahedron sculpture was born out of Jonathan Doane's deep appreciation for the Math Department and his desire to leave a lasting legacy. The Math Alliance Club, under his leadership, aimed to explore mathematics in new, fun, and engaging ways. This project was the perfect vehicle to fulfill that mission.

Proposal and Approval

Gaining Institutional Support

Securing approval for the sculpture was no small feat. Jonathan and the Math Alliance Club worked tirelessly to gain the support of President Esterberg's Council. This involved ongoing communication to ensure that the project aligned with the university's standards and vision.

Collaboration with Physical Plant

Building the Foundation

One of the critical aspects of the project was coordinating with SUNY Potsdam's Physical Plant. The team worked closely with the Physical Plant staff to ensure that the cement pad and icosahedron sculpture were built to code. This involved meticulous planning, obtaining necessary approvals, and drafting a contract to declare ownership and maintenance responsibilities.

Pictured: SUNY Potsdam's Physical Plant laying down the foundational cement pad.

Volunteer Efforts and Construction

Hands-On Collaboration

Under the guidance of sculptor Alicia Quijano, the Math Alliance Club members and volunteers dedicated countless hours to the construction of the sculpture. The process involved drilling pilot holes, securely bolting custom joints to the cement pad, and welding the intricate components together. Each member played a crucial role, showcasing their skills and commitment to the project.

Inspiration from the Icosahedron Sculpture

A Student's Reflection on Choosing SUNY Potsdam

An excerpt from an FY 100 student at SUNY Potsdam, as reported to me by Dr. Joel Foisy in September of 2018:

"Above is a picture [not included, here] of me with the icosahedron outside of Carson Hall. Its role is to remind us that mathematics professors and students of SUNY Potsdam are held to the highest standard they can be held to. It’s been around since only 2015, and I don’t know if that’s enough time for it to have given anyone else inspiration. What I do know, however, is that it inspired me. During my first visit to this campus in 2016, I noticed this. I went up and read the associated marker. I don’t know what it was, but something about it struck me. Before, I was considering many colleges. A lot of my friends were going to UB, Binghamton, or Corning Community College. I would be the only one here if I chose Potsdam. From the moment I read that sign, I knew that this would be my first choice."

Legacy and Impact

Inspiring Future Generations

Today, the icosahedron sculpture stands proudly outside Carson Hall at SUNY Potsdam. It serves as a lasting reminder of the Math Alliance Club's commitment to innovation and community. The project not only beautified the campus but also left an indelible mark on the Math Department, inspiring future generations of students to pursue their passions with dedication and creativity.

Pictured: The icosahedron sculpture outside of Carson Hall, being enjoyed by (hopefully) future mathematicians.

Conclusion

A Testament to Dedication

The icosahedron sculpture project was more than just a mathematical endeavor; it was a journey of collaboration, perseverance, and impact. Jonathan Doane and the Math Alliance Club demonstrated that with passion and teamwork, even the most ambitious projects can become a reality. This story is a testament to their unorthodox level of dedication and innovation, leaving a lasting legacy at SUNY Potsdam.
